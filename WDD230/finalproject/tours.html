Things to do in Cozumel

El Cielo

Imagine immersing yourself in the middle of the crystal clear waters of the Riviera Maya and find out that the sky can also be under the sea. How is that possible? Well, if you take a boat ride to surround this wonderful island, it will take you to El Cielo beach where this peculiar natural phenomenon takes place. In addition to having crystal clear waters and very fine white sand starfish can usually be found. When you submerge yourself you will feel as if you have the sky under your feet with a -literally- starry seabed.

It’s important to do these routes with a certified guide and be especially careful not to touch this specie and also not to take it out of the water because, in a few minutes, it could die and the ecosystem will be severely affected.

Thanks to the tranquility of the sea and its lack of waves, this place becomes one of the favorites to snorkel. If you look closely, it is possible to find stingrays that will swim close to you, but be careful not to disturb them or to step on them, this way you can help to preserve the natural landscape and local fauna.

Take advantage of the day by getting relaxed and lying down under the sun to forget about the routine. The tour lasts 30 minutes by boat on the open sea.

San Gervasio archeological site

In your visit to Cozumel Quintana Roo, a tour to the archaeological area of San Gervasio, is a must. This is one of the most studied Pre-Hispanic centers in the island of Cozumel. Settled in the place where the old Rancho San Gervasio used to be, this ceremonial complex also served as a worship center for the moon, love, gestation and medicine goddess: Ixchel.

The archeological complex, dating from 100 BC, has different areas to explore: Chichán Nah, El Álamo, Nohoch Nah, Murciélagos or Ka'na Nah. The sacbés, or white sand roads, will take you to each one of these places. While you go through the area, imagine how the life of the Mayan people wouldhad been, try to imagine their every day activities and they religious ceremonies.

The Arco of San Gervasio in Cozumel is one of the most visited places of this archaeological area because it is very similar to the ones that are in Chichén Itzá, Kabáh or Labná. If you are looking for the strongest connection with nature you can head for La Casa Alta or Ka Nah and visit the cenote there.

The best option to get to this archaeological area in Cozumel is by getting a taxi or a car. It is open to public every day from 8 AM to 4:30 PM.

Adventure Park Chankanaab

Located in the Arrecifes Cozumel National Park, the Chankanaab Adventure Park is ideal to enjoy the tranquility of the waters of this Mexican Caribbean destination, as well as to practice snorkeling or diving between reefs.

There is entertainment for the whole family, as it has an eco-archaeological tour, encounters with sea lions, crocodiles and manatees, as well as guided snuba and seatrek tours. To add a gastronomic touch, you can have a tequila experience and get away from stress in its temazcal.

Museum of Cozumel

Recently renovated, at the Cozumel Museum you will discover the origin of the destination, its natural diversity and the stories of its inhabitants.

It has 11 interactive rooms with a permanent exhibition and 2 temporary rooms that house the work of local and international artists, as well as a room dedicated entirely to the Cozumel Carnival.

The building that houses the museum was built in the 1930s and served as one of the first hotels in Cozumel.
